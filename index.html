What Atlas Is

Atlas is a hardened enforcement gate that validates action intent, enforces written policy, and stops risky commands before they execute. Every decision is captured in a tamper-resistant audit trail so clients can prove compliance and investigate incidents with confidence.

How Atlas Reduces Risk

  • Prevents unauthorized actions by enforcing policy before execution

  • Provides full auditability for compliance, incident response, and war-room review

Technical Architecture

Atlas operates as a TPM-bound sidecar proxy that verifies runtime actions, generates attestation evidence, and enforces policy before execution.

  • Hardware binding to TPM 2.0 for immutable trust anchors
  • Immutable audit trails for every transaction
  • Fail-closed enforcement for unauthorized actions

How Atlas Works

  • Loads a policy manifest from policy.json
  • Receives an action and justification
  • Validates semantic anchors, rationale threshold, and required classes
  • Returns a deterministic decision

Decision Outcomes

Authorized

The action is approved and may proceed safely under enforced policy.

FailClosed

The action is rejected without execution, preserving safety and halting risk propagation.

HardwareKillState

The action is blocked at the highest severity and the runtime is isolated to prevent further damage.
